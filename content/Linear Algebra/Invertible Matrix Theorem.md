Let $\mathbf{M}$ be an $n \times n$ matrix, and let $\mathcal{T}: \mathbb{R}^n \rightarrow \mathbb{R}^n$ be the matrix transformation $T(\mathbf{v}) = \mathbf{Mv}$. The following statements are equivalent:
1. $\mathbf{M}$ is invertible.
2. $\mathbf{M}$ has $n$ pivots.
3. $\text{Nul}(\mathbf{M})=\{\mathbf{0}\}$
4. The columns of $\mathbf{M}$ are *linearly independent*.
5. The columns of $\mathbf{M}$ span $\mathbb{R}^n$.
6. $\mathbf{Mv}=\mathbf{a}$ has a unique solution for each $\mathbf{a}$ in $\mathbb{R}^n$.
7. $T$ is invertible
