Assuming a model $X \sim P_\theta$ with a fixed, but unknown parameter $\theta$ (frequentist assumption), the Cramer-Rao Lower Bound (CRLB) provides a lower bound on the variance of an estimator $T(X)$.


Note: It is possible to achieve lower variance than the lower bound if the estimator is biased (See [[Bias-Variance Tradeoff]]).
